General Population OverView Of Rwimiyaga Sector

Total Number of Population In Rwimiyaga Sector

Rwimiyaga is a sector located in Nyagatare District, within Rwanda's Eastern Province. According to the 2022 Rwanda Population and Housing Census, Rwimiyaga has a population of 82,620 residents, with 40,685 males and 41,935 females, making it one of the more populous sectors in Nyagatare District.

Population Density

The sector spans an area of approximately 309 km², resulting in a population density of about 267 inhabitants per km². This density is lower compared to some neighboring sectors, such as Karama, Gatunda, Mimuli, and Rukomo, which have densities exceeding 500 inhabitants per km².

Population Growth Rate

Over the past decade, Rwimiyaga has experienced significant population growth. Between 2012 and 2022, the population increased from 57,527 to 82,620, representing an annual growth rate of approximately 3.7%. This growth is part of a broader trend in Nyagatare District, where sectors like Rwimiyaga, Karangazi, and Nyagatare have seen their populations more than double in recent years.

Age Of Population

The median age in Rwimiyaga is relatively young, with both males and females having a median age of approximately 16.6 years. This youthful demographic is consistent with the broader trends observed in Nyagatare District and the Eastern Province, reflecting a high fertility rate and a predominantly young population

Geographically Location

google Map of Rwimiyaga Sector

Geographically, Rwimiyaga shares borders with Karangazi to the north, Nyagatare to the west, and Rwempasha to the northwest. The Kagera River, which forms part of Rwanda's border with Tanzania, flows along the sector's northern edge, providing a natural boundary and contributing to the area's agricultural significance

Cells of Rwimiyaga Sector

The sector is administratively divided into eight cells: Gacundezi, Kabeza, Kirebe, Ntoma, Nyarupfubire, Nyendo, Rutungu, and Rwimiyaga. These divisions facilitate local governance and service delivery, aiming to address the needs of the growing population effectively

Birth Rate

Rwanda’s birth rate has been gradually decreasing over the years, thanks to widespread family planning initiatives and increasing public awareness of reproductive health. The government’s efforts in promoting maternal health and education have contributed to this decline, although the country still experiences a relatively high birth rate compared to global averages.

Migration And Population Movement

Migration within Rwanda has been primarily rural-to-urban, as many people move from the countryside to cities like Kigali in search of better employment opportunities and living conditions. This population movement is driven by the desire for improved education, healthcare, and economic prospects. The urbanization trend continues as the country’s cities grow rapidly.

Employment Rate

Rwanda’s employment rate has seen improvement, particularly in sectors like agriculture, construction, and services. The government has made efforts to create job opportunities through infrastructure projects, small and medium enterprise support, and education programs. However, underemployment remains a concern, especially in rural areas where many people are engaged in subsistence farming or informal jobs.

Literacy Rate

Rwanda boasts a high literacy rate, with significant strides made in education over the last few decades. The government has prioritized education, which has resulted in an increasing number of people, particularly women, gaining access to primary and secondary education. Literacy rates continue to improve as the country invests in education at all levels.

Life Expectancy

Rwanda’s life expectancy has improved significantly over the past few decades, largely due to advances in healthcare, nutrition, and sanitation. With improved access to medical care, life expectancy has risen, and the country has made notable progress in reducing child mortality rates and addressing infectious diseases like malaria and HIV/AIDS.

Population Below Line of Poverty

Despite the country’s economic growth, a significant portion of Rwanda’s population still lives below the poverty line. Poverty remains a challenge, particularly in rural areas, where many people rely on subsistence farming and face limited access to services. The government has been implementing policies aimed at poverty reduction, but the gap between rich and poor continues to be a concern.

In Short

Rwimiyaga is a rapidly growing sector with a youthful population, significant population density, and strategic geographical features that influence its development and integration within Nyagatare District and the broader Eastern Province.
