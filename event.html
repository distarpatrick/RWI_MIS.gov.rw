Kwita Izina Ingagi

Kwita Izina Kwita Izina is an annual ceremony held in Rwandan dedicated to naming newborn mountain gorillas. Inspired by the Rwandan tradition of naming babies, this event underscores Rwanda's commitment to conservation and ecotourism. It not only celebrates the endangered mountain gorillas but also raises global awareness about their protection. International figures, conservationists, and local communities participate in the event, showcasing Rwanda’s environmental stewardship.

Itorero

Itorero Itorero, meaning "school" or "assembly," refers to cultural and civic education programs in Rwanda. Historically, Itorero played a key role in teaching young people Rwandan values, history, and skills. Today, the concept has been revitalized as a platform for promoting unity, patriotism, and leadership among Rwandans. It aims to instill a strong sense of identity and community through education, dialogue, and national service activities.

Umuganda

Umuganda Umuganda is a monthly community service initiative in Rwanda, held on the last Saturday of each month. The term translates to "coming together for a common purpose," and it embodies the spirit of collective effort. During Umuganda, citizens gather to clean streets, build infrastructure, plant trees, and address local challenges. This practice fosters social cohesion and contributes to Rwanda’s sustainable development.

Umuganura

. Umuganura Umuganura is a traditional Rwandan festival celebrating the harvest and national unity. Once a royal tradition, it marked the end of the harvest season and expressed gratitude for the land's bounty. Today, Umuganura has been revived as a national holiday to honor Rwanda's agricultural heritage and achievements. It emphasizes the importance of hard work, collaboration, and cultural identity in ensuring the nation's prosperity.

Kwibuka Kwibuka means "to remember," and it refers to Rwanda’s annual commemoration of the 1994 Genocide against the Tutsi. Over a 100-day period, activities focus on honoring the victims, supporting survivors, and educating about the tragedy. Kwibuka promotes healing, reconciliation, and the prevention of genocide, ensuring that the world never forgets this dark chapter in history and works towards peace and understanding.
